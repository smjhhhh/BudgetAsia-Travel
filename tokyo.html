Tokyo

A mesmerizing metropolis where ancient traditions seamlessly blend with cutting-edge technology, neon lights illuminate serene shrines, and every corner reveals a new discovery.

Plan Your Trip

Why Tokyo?

Tokyo is a city of endless fascination, where tradition and innovation coexist in perfect harmony. From the tranquil gardens of the Imperial Palace to the electric energy of Shibuya Crossing, every neighborhood tells a different story.

Despite its reputation as an expensive city, Tokyo offers numerous budget-friendly options. Affordable conveyor belt sushi restaurants, free temple visits, and an efficient public transportation system make it possible to experience this incredible city without overspending. The city's safety, cleanliness, and exceptional service ensure a stress-free travel experience.

Top Attractions

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest temple in Asakusa, featuring the iconic Thunder Gate and bustling Nakamise shopping street.

Shibuya Crossing

Experience the world's busiest pedestrian crossing, where thousands navigate in perfect organized chaos.

Meiji Shrine

A tranquil Shinto shrine surrounded by lush forest, offering peaceful respite from the urban energy.

Must-Try Japanese Cuisine

Tokyo's food scene ranges from Michelin-starred restaurants to humble ramen shops. Discover authentic flavors that won't empty your wallet.

Ramen

Rich noodle soup with various broths, topped with pork, egg, and vegetables.

Sushi

Fresh fish on vinegared rice, best enjoyed at conveyor belt restaurants for budget travelers.

Takoyaki

Savory octopus-filled batter balls topped with sauce, mayo, and bonito flakes.

Tonkatsu

Breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet served with shredded cabbage and savory sauce.

3-Day Tokyo Itinerary

Immerse yourself in Tokyo's perfect blend of ancient tradition and futuristic innovation with this comprehensive 3-day guide.

1

Day 1: Traditional Tokyo

Temples, Gardens & Historic Districts

Landmarks

  • Morning:

    Senso-ji Temple, Asakusa

    Walk through the iconic Thunder Gate (Kaminarimon), browse Nakamise shopping street, and visit Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple.

  • Afternoon:

    Imperial Palace East Gardens

    Explore the free public gardens of the Imperial Palace, featuring traditional Japanese landscaping and historic fortifications.

  • Evening:

    Meiji Shrine & Harajuku

    Visit the serene Shinto shrine in a forested sanctuary, then experience Harajuku's vibrant street fashion culture on Takeshita Street.

Food

  • Breakfast:

    Traditional Japanese breakfast set at a local café near Asakusa (~¥800)

  • Lunch:

    Tempura donburi at Daikokuya Tempura in Asakusa (~¥1,200)

  • Dinner:

    Conveyor belt sushi at Uobei Shibuya Dogenzaka (~¥1,500)

2

Day 2: Modern Tokyo

Technology, Pop Culture & Neon Nights

Landmarks

  • Morning:

    Tsukiji Outer Market

    Sample fresh seafood, tamagoyaki, and street food at Tokyo's famous fish market (arrive by 8 AM for best selection).

  • Afternoon:

    Akihabara Electric Town

    Dive into otaku culture with anime shops, electronics stores, maid cafés, and retro gaming arcades.

  • Evening:

    Shibuya Crossing & Shinjuku

    Experience the world's busiest intersection, then explore Shinjuku's neon-lit Kabukicho district and view city lights from Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building (free!).

Food

  • Breakfast:

    Fresh uni (sea urchin) and tuna sashimi at Tsukiji Outer Market (~¥1,500)

  • Lunch:

    Tonkatsu set at Maisen in Harajuku (~¥1,400)

  • Dinner:

    Ramen at Ichiran Shibuya, famous solo dining booths (~¥980)

3

Day 3: Day Trip & Local Neighborhoods

Nature, Art & Hidden Gems

Landmarks

  • Morning:

    Kamakura Day Trip: Great Buddha

    Take a 1-hour train to Kamakura to see the 13-meter bronze Buddha statue and explore historic Zen temples (optional: return for afternoon in Tokyo).

  • Afternoon:

    Odaiba Seaside Park

    Visit the futuristic island with teamLab Borderless digital art museum, life-size Gundam statue, and Rainbow Bridge views.

  • Evening:

    Shimokitazawa Neighborhood

    Wander through Tokyo's hippest neighborhood with vintage shops, indie theaters, and cozy izakayas.

Food

  • Breakfast:

    Onigiri and coffee from 7-Eleven or Family Mart (~¥400)

  • Lunch:

    Shirasu-don (whitebait rice bowl) in Kamakura (~¥1,200)

  • Dinner:

    Yakitori and sake at a local izakaya in Shimokitazawa (~¥2,500)

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March-May) for cherry blossoms or autumn (September-November) for fall foliage. Summer can be hot and humid, winter is cold but less crowded.

Budget Tips

Purchase a JR Pass if traveling beyond Tokyo. Convenience stores offer quality meals at low prices. Many museums offer free admission days.

Cultural Etiquette

Bow when greeting. Remove shoes in traditional spaces. Keep voices low on trains. Cash is still widely used, so carry yen.

Getting Around

The subway system is extensive and efficient. Get a Suica or Pasmo card for seamless travel. Google Maps works perfectly for navigation.
