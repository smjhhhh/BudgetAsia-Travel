South Korea

Seoul

South Korea's captivating capital where K-pop culture thrives alongside ancient palaces, trendy cafes neighbor traditional hanok villages, and innovation meets tradition at every turn.

Plan Your Trip

Why Seoul?

Seoul is a city that never sleeps, offering an intoxicating blend of ultra-modern skyscrapers, Buddhist temples, and royal palaces. The birthplace of K-pop and Korean Wave culture, Seoul has become a global trendsetter in music, fashion, beauty, and technology.

Budget travelers will find Seoul surprisingly affordable, with excellent public transportation, countless free attractions, and affordable yet delicious street food. The city's safe environment, fast Wi-Fi everywhere, and friendly locals make it an ideal destination for first-time travelers to Asia.

Top Attractions

Gyeongbokgung Palace

The largest of Five Grand Palaces, witness the changing of the guard ceremony at this stunning royal complex.

Bukchon Hanok Village

Wander through traditional Korean houses nestled between palaces, offering glimpse into historic Seoul.

Myeongdong Shopping

Seoul's premier shopping district for Korean cosmetics, fashion, and street food in a vibrant atmosphere.

Must-Try Korean Food

Korean cuisine is bold, flavorful, and incredibly diverse. From sizzling barbecue to comforting stews, Seoul offers culinary adventures at every price point.

Bibimbap

Mixed rice bowl with vegetables, meat, egg, and gochujang sauce.

Korean BBQ

Grilled marinated beef or pork, wrapped in lettuce with sauces and sides.

Tteokbokki

Spicy stir-fried rice cakes in sweet and spicy gochujang sauce.

Kimchi Jjigae

Hearty kimchi stew with tofu, pork, and vegetables in savory broth.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April-May) for cherry blossoms or autumn (September-November) for comfortable weather and fall colors. Avoid summer humidity and winter cold.

Budget Tips

Many palaces offer free admission in traditional hanbok dress. Convenience stores have affordable meals. Use T-money card for discounted public transit.

Cultural Etiquette

Remove shoes before entering homes. Use both hands when giving or receiving. Respect hierarchical social structure. Tipping is not customary.

Getting Around

The subway system is modern, efficient, and affordable. Get a T-money card at any convenience store. Kakao Map app is essential for navigation.
